GPA Calculation Rule

1. At Ilia State University, the GPA (Grade Point Average) is the quotient obtained by dividing the sum of the products of the Grade Points (GP) earned by the student in each course and the credits of that course, by the sum of the credits of all the courses the student has completed:

GPA = Σ(GP of each course × credits of each course) / Σ(credits of each course)

2. GP (Grade Point) is the grade obtained in a single course, converted onto a 4-point scale.

3. Grade categories (GP system)

  • F — 0–50 points (0 points, Fail)
  • E — 51–60 points
  • D — 61–70 points
  • C — 71–80 points
  • B — 81–90 points
  • A — 91–95 points
  • A+ — 96–100 points

4. Under the GP system, within each course:

  • an E grade (51–60) is fixed at 1.00 GP;
  • D, C, B, A grades (from 61 to 95 points) increase uniformly by 1/12 (≈0.08) for every point;
  • an A+ grade (from 96 to 100 points) is fixed and equals 4.00 GP.

The fixed ("frozen") GP values inside the E and A+ ranges mean that differences between the scores within those intervals are not significant: any score of 51–60 is considered equally low, and any score of 96–100 is considered equally outstanding.

5. The GPA is awarded for all academic programs, except at the doctoral level. It is also not awarded for certificate programs.

6. Only the final GPA is issued and it is recorded in the diploma supplement. This does not apply to the single-stage Medical Doctor program.

7. When computing the final GPA, fractions are rounded to the nearest tenth.
